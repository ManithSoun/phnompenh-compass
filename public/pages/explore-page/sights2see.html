Sights to See in Phnom Penh

From majestic palaces to bustling markets, discover the iconic landmarks and hidden gems that define Phnom Penh's unique character.

Khan Daun Penh

Royal Palace

The official residence of the King of Cambodia. A stunning example of Khmer architecture with golden spires.

$10 8AM - 11AM, 2PM - 5PM
(4.9)
Wat Phnom

Wat Phnom Temple

The namesake temple on a small hill, founded in 1372. The birthplace of Phnom Penh's legend.

$1 7AM - 6PM
(4.6)
Central Market

Central Market (Phsar Thmei)

Art Deco landmark built in 1937. Shop for jewelry, clothing, souvenirs, and local delicacies.

Free 7AM - 5PM
(4.5)
Sisowath Quay

Riverside Promenade

Scenic walkway along the Tonle Sap River. Perfect for evening strolls with restaurants and street vendors.

Free Open 24/7
(4.8)
Toul Tom Poung

Russian Market (Phsar Toul Tom Poung)

Famous for bargains on clothing, antiques, and handicrafts. Great place to practice haggling skills.

Free 7AM - 5PM
(4.4)
Daun Penh

Independence Monument

Built in 1958 to commemorate independence from France. Beautifully illuminated at night.

Free Open 24/7
(4.7)
Koh Pich

Diamond Island

Modern development with parks, entertainment, and river views. Great for cycling and family activities.

Free Open Daily
(4.2)
Street 136

Night Market

Vibrant evening market with street food, crafts, and live entertainment. Opens after sunset.

Free 6PM - 12AM
(4.6)
Chamkarmon

Olympic Stadium

Historic sports complex where locals gather for exercise. Join morning aerobics or evening runs.

Free 5AM - 9PM
(4.1)
Street 240

Kandal Market

Authentic local market with fresh produce, seafood, and street food. Experience daily Khmer life.

Free 5AM - 6PM
(4.8)
BKK1

Aeon Mall

Modern shopping complex with international brands, cinema, and food court. Air-conditioned escape.

Free 9AM - 10PM
(4.5)
Boeng Keng Kang

Boeng Keng Kang Lake

Peaceful lake surrounded by parks and cafes. Popular spot for morning exercise and sunset views.

Free Open 24/7
(4.7)
